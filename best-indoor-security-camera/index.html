Frequently asked questions

Where should I place indoor security cameras?

The most useful placements are entry points (front door, back door, garage entry), main living areas where you can see multiple rooms from a single angle, and anywhere you keep valuables. Avoid pointing cameras at bedrooms or bathrooms. For a first camera, the front door entry area is almost always the most actionable placement: it covers the most common point of entry and the footage is most likely to be useful after an incident. Corner placements at ceiling height give the widest field of view; low-angle tabletop placements give more face-level coverage in entryways.

Can indoor cameras also work outdoors?

Not reliably. Indoor cameras lack the weatherproofing (IP rating) that outdoor cameras need to survive rain, humidity, temperature swings, and direct sunlight. Using an indoor camera outside will likely work fine until it doesn’t, and moisture damage usually isn’t covered under warranty when a camera was used outside its rated environment. If you need a camera that works in a covered outdoor space like a porch, some cameras (like the Google Nest Cam) come in versions explicitly rated for outdoor use in protected spots. Get the right tool for the location.

Do indoor cameras need to be plugged in?

Most indoor cameras are plug-in (AC power), which is practical indoors since outlets are plentiful and continuous recording doesn’t drain a battery. Battery-powered indoor cameras exist and are useful if you want to move the camera between rooms or don’t have a convenient outlet, but they run on the same batteries as their outdoor counterparts and will need more frequent charging if they’re capturing a lot of indoor motion. The Wyze Cam v4 is a plug-in camera and its constant power is one of its strengths for indoor reliability.
