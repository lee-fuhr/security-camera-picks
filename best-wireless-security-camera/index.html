Frequently asked questions

How long do wireless security camera batteries last?

Battery life varies widely by camera, settings, and how much motion the camera captures. On a typical residential driveway seeing 10 to 20 motion events per day, most wire-free cameras last between one and six months on a full charge. The Arlo Pro 5S is on the shorter side of that range but compensates with better video quality and faster motion response. Cameras like the Eufy SoloCam with a solar panel can go indefinitely with adequate direct sunlight. Key battery drain factors: high resolution video, color night vision (spotlight use), frequent motion events, and cold temperatures. Set detection zones to reduce false triggers and you’ll see noticeably longer battery life.

Do wireless cameras work if Wi-Fi goes down?

No for remote access and alerts, but some cameras continue to record locally during a Wi-Fi outage. Cameras with on-board SD card storage (Reolink Argus, Eufy SoloCam) will keep recording to the card even when the internet is down; you can review that footage once connectivity resumes. Cameras that rely solely on cloud storage (older Ring models without a plan, some Arlo configurations) will miss events during an outage entirely. For reliable coverage during internet outages, choose a camera with local storage as a fallback, and consider a cellular backup router for critical monitoring locations.

Can wireless cameras be hacked?

Like any internet-connected device, wireless cameras carry some risk, but it’s manageable with reasonable precautions. Use a camera from an established brand with a record of releasing firmware updates. Connect cameras through a strong, unique Wi-Fi password and consider putting them on a separate guest network or IoT VLAN so a compromised camera can’t reach other devices. Enable two-factor authentication on your camera account. Avoid cameras from no-name manufacturers with no firmware update history, especially for cameras that have a view into your home’s interior. The risk with reputable brands is low if you follow basic security hygiene.
